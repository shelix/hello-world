Ny = 20
Tmax = 100
emax = 10
y = np.zeros((Tmax,Ny))
for i in range(0,Ny-1):
    for j in range(0,Tmax-1):
        y[j+1,i]=y[j,i]+rand(-emax,emax)
    plt.plot(y)

At a later point in time, the value of each random variable is a sum of randomly generated numbers. So by the Central Limit Theorum, the distribution of all the values at a later point in time should approach the normal distribution. Let's try it, but with larger numbers:

In [67]:
Ny = 100
Tmax = 500
emax = 10
y = np.zeros((Tmax,Ny))
for i in range(0,Ny-1):
    for j in range(0,Tmax-1):
        y[j+1,i]=y[j,i]+rand(-emax,emax)
plt.show(plt.hist(y[100]))

Cool, that looks kind of normal!
